Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about residential electrical services

Residential electrical service costs vary depending on the job. Simple repairs like replacing an outlet typically range from $75-150, while larger projects like rewiring a room can range from $500-2,000. We provide free estimates for all projects so you know exactly what to expect.

Outlets that keep tripping are usually GFCI (Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter) outlets protecting against ground faults. Common causes include moisture near the outlet, overloaded circuits, faulty appliances, or underlying wiring issues. If your outlets trip frequently, it's best to have an electrician inspect the circuit to identify the root cause.

Signs your home may need rewiring include: frequent breaker trips, flickering or dimming lights, burning smells from outlets or switches, discolored or warm outlets, homes built over 40 years ago with original wiring, or if your home has aluminum wiring. A licensed electrician can assess your wiring and recommend solutions.

In Utah, permits are required for most electrical work beyond simple repairs like replacing a light fixture or outlet. Adding circuits, installing new wiring, panel work, and major repairs typically require permits. As licensed electricians, we handle all permit requirements and inspections for you.
